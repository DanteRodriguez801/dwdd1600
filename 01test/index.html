Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship means using technology, the internet, and online communication responsibly, respectfully, safely, and legally.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law is the set of legal rules that govern how people use technology and online content. It helps protect people's rights, privacy, property, and safety in digital spaces.

What is AUP?

An AUP, or Acceptable Use Policy, is a document that explains the rules for using technology and the internet responsibly in a school, workplace, or organization.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism means using someone else's words, ideas, work, images, code, music, or designs and presenting them as your own without proper credit.

Plagiarism is basically academic theft. Even if it is accidental, schools still treat it seriously.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty means cheating, lying, plagiarizing, or using unfair methods to complete schoolwork.

Academic dishonesty is any action that gives a student an unfair academic advantage or misrepresents their work as honest and original.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

Rules for AI-generated content are the guidelines for how you are allowed to use tools like ChatGPT, image generators, code generators, or other AI tools in schoolwork.

AI-generated content may be used if the instructor allows it, but students must give proper credit, understand the work they submit, and make sure the final assignment follows academic honesty rules. Using AI without credit can still count as plagiarism.
