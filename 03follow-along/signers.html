Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson
Thomas Jefferson was the main author of the Declaration of Independence. His writing explained why the colonies believed they had the right to separate from Great Britain.
John Adams
John Adams was a strong supporter of independence. He helped convince other colonial leaders that breaking away from Great Britain was necessary.
Benjamin Franklin
Benjamin Franklin was a respected writer, inventor, scientist, and diplomat. He was one of the most well-known leaders involved in the founding of the United States.
John Hancock
John Hancock was the president of the Second Continental Congress. He is famous for signing the Declaration with a large, bold signature.
Samuel Adams
Samuel Adams was a political leader who helped encourage resistance against British rule. He played an important role in building support for independence.
Richard Henry Lee
Richard Henry Lee was a Virginia statesman who introduced the resolution calling for independence from Great Britain. His proposal helped lead to the creation of the Declaration.
Roger Sherman
Roger Sherman was a signer from Connecticut. He is known for being involved in several important founding documents of the United States.
Robert Morris
Robert Morris was a signer from Pennsylvania. He helped support the American Revolution through his work with money, trade, and supplies.

Together, these signers helped turn the colonies’ desire for independence into an official statement to the world. Their decision was risky because signing the Declaration could have been viewed as treason by Great Britain. Today, many of their names are remembered because they helped shape the beginning of the United States and supported the idea that people have rights that government should protect.
