What Is Survival Horror

Understanding the Genre

Survival horror is a type of video game that focuses on fear, survival, and tension. The player is usually placed in a dangerous location with limited supplies, difficult enemies, and a strong feeling of being unsafe. Instead of giving the player unlimited weapons or healing items, survival horror makes every choice feel important.

The genre works because it creates pressure. A locked door, a strange sound, or a nearly empty inventory can make the player feel nervous before anything even attacks. Good survival horror uses atmosphere, pacing, and resource management to keep the player uncomfortable.

Important Survival Horror Companies

Several companies helped survival horror become popular by creating games that used fear, limited resources, and dangerous environments in different ways.

  • Capcom, known for Resident Evil and horror action gameplay.
  • Konami, known for Silent Hill and psychological horror storytelling.
  • Frictional Games, known for Amnesia and first-person horror experiences.
  • Red Barrels, known for Outlast and intense chase-based horror gameplay.

Common Survival Horror Terms

Survival horror games often use special design ideas that make the player feel vulnerable. These terms help explain some of the most common parts of the genre.

Inventory Management
Inventory management means choosing which weapons, healing items, keys, and supplies to carry because space is limited.
Safe Room
A safe room is an area where the player can usually save progress, store items, or take a short break from danger.
Limited Resources
Limited resources means the game does not give the player unlimited ammunition, health items, or supplies. This forces the player to make careful decisions.

Beginner-Friendly Examples

Players who are new to survival horror may want to begin with games that are scary but still fair. Some games focus more on action, while others focus more on puzzles, atmosphere, or running from danger.

  1. Resident Evil 2 Remake
  2. Resident Evil 4 Remake
  3. Silent Hill 2 Remake
  4. Amnesia: The Dark Descent
  5. Outlast

Survival horror is not only about jump scares. The strongest games in the genre make the player feel uncertain about what is coming next. That uncertainty is what keeps the experience tense, especially when the player feels alone and unprepared.
