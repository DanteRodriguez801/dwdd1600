Survival Horror Pioneers

Survival horror was shaped by several important creators, games, and companies. The genre did not come from one person alone. It grew from a mix of horror storytelling, puzzle design, limited resources, and technology that made scary environments feel more immersive.

Shinji Mikami
Shinji Mikami is closely connected to the rise of survival horror because he directed the original Resident Evil. His work helped establish many familiar genre features, including limited ammunition, tense exploration, puzzles, and dangerous enemies.
Keiichiro Toyama
Keiichiro Toyama directed the original Silent Hill. His work helped push survival horror toward psychological fear, disturbing atmosphere, foggy environments, and mystery-driven storytelling.
Tokuro Fujiwara
Tokuro Fujiwara helped produce Sweet Home, an earlier horror game that influenced many ideas later seen in survival horror. These ideas included exploration, locked areas, limited items, and a frightening mansion setting.

Important Survival Horror Companies

Several companies helped survival horror become popular. Some focused on monsters and action, while others focused on psychological fear, helplessness, or disturbing environments.

  • Capcom, known for Resident Evil and other horror action games.
  • Konami, known for Silent Hill and psychological horror storytelling.
  • Frictional Games, known for Amnesia and first-person horror experiences.
  • Red Barrels, known for Outlast and intense chase-based horror gameplay.
